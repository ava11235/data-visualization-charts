Common visualizations with practical use cases

Line Chart

Use Case: Track trends over time such as stock prices, website traffic, temperature changes, or sales performance across months/years.

Bar Chart

Use Case: Compare categorical data like sales by product, revenue by region, or survey responses across different groups.

Pie Chart

Use Case: Show proportions or percentages of a whole, such as market share distribution, budget allocation, or demographic breakdowns.

Doughnut Chart

Use Case: Similar to pie charts but with better readability for displaying composition of datasets like customer segments or resource utilization.

Scatter Plot

Use Case: Identify correlations and relationships between two variables like height vs weight, advertising spend vs sales, or test scores vs study hours.

Area Chart

Use Case: Emphasize magnitude of change over time, useful for cumulative data like total revenue growth or resource consumption patterns.

Horizontal Bar Chart

Use Case: Compare items with long labels, rankings, or when you have many categories like top 10 products or employee performance scores.

Horizontal Percentage Bar Chart

Use Case: Display completion rates, satisfaction percentages, or progress metrics across categories like project completion or survey agreement levels.

Stacked Bar Chart

Use Case: Show part-to-whole relationships over categories, like sales breakdown by product line across quarters or demographic distribution by region.

Radar Chart

Use Case: Compare multiple quantitative variables simultaneously, ideal for performance metrics, skill assessments, or competitive analysis across dimensions.

Polar Area Chart

Use Case: Display cyclical data with varying magnitudes, such as seasonal patterns, directional data, or periodic measurements like wind patterns.

Bubble Chart

Use Case: Visualize three dimensions of data simultaneously, such as GDP (x) vs life expectancy (y) vs population (size) across countries.

Multi-Line Chart

Use Case: Compare multiple trends simultaneously over time, like comparing performance of different products, teams, or A/B test variants.
